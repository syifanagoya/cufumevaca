Ten Percent Season 1 Recap:

The show takes place in Nightingale Hart, a talent agency located in London, and revolves around the lives of the agents and their famous clients. In the first season, the agency faces a major upheaval when its founder, Richard Nightingale, unexpectedly passes away. As a result, Jonathan Nightingale, Rebecca Fox, Stella Hart, and Dan Bala, along with the rest of the team, must navigate the aftermath of this loss while also dealing with their own personal and professional difficulties.

Adding to the complexity, Misha, Jonathan’s daughter from an extramarital affair, becomes an assistant at the agency right as her father’s death occurs. Jonathan and Stella soon discover that the agency is encountering financial troubles, and they must work tirelessly to keep it afloat. Meanwhile, Rebecca endeavors to establish a production branch within the agency while simultaneously developing feelings for Margaux, the author of the book she plans to adapt. Dan, on the other hand, attempts to launch the acting career of Zoe, their former receptionist, while also falling for her.

Throughout their journey, the agents must also contend with their demanding celebrity clients while warding off the influence of the American company that acquired the agency.

Ten Percent Season 1 Review:

The first season of Ten Percent has received a variety of reviews from critics. As per Rotten Tomatoes, the show has achieved a Tomatometer score of 92%. Some critics have commended the series for its exceptional cast and inclusion of celebrity guest stars, while others have expressed their dissatisfaction, deeming it an inadequate adaptation of the original French show, Call My Agent!.

Ten Percent Season 1 Rating:
