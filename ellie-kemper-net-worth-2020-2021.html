Early life

Ellie Kemper’s father David woods Kemper was the chairman and the C.E.O of Commerce Bancshares, a bank holding company founded by members of Kemper’s family a few generations earlier. Her maternal grandfather was Italian; however, from her father’s side, she is English. She even has French and German ancestry. Although her place of birth was Kansas, Missouri, they had to move to St. Louise while she was five years old.

At the elementary level, she studied at the Conway school located in the posh suburban region of Ladue. And later she went to John Burroughs High school. It is in her high school days that she developed an interest in theatre and acting. At this stage, she got an opportunity to appear in a school play with actor John Hamm, who was one of the teachers at the institute. After high school, she got her degree in English from Princeton University.

During her first year at Princeton, she played in the hockey team which even reached the national competition that year.

Career

Ellie Kemper’s career had virtually started while she was in Princeton itself. During this time she was active in the improvisational comedy of different sorts. She was reportedly involved with Quipfire, -an ancient stand up comedy group based in Princeton and also the Princeton triangle club.

However, Ellie’s television career started with a commercial for Kmart. She started performing on shows, like “Late Night with Conan O’Brien.” She has also appeared on several comedy shows and has even performed sketches. Apart from acting, she has been actively involved in writing. She has written various sketches, mostly with Scott Eckert, who has been with Ellie Kemper since her days at Princeton.

She has been published in various satirical newspapers, like the Onion, Mc Sweeny’s and the Huffington Post, to name a few. Ever since she moved to New York, she has been vigorously involved with the Upright citizen’s brigade. She has done several shows and performances with them including the one-woman comedy show, “Dumb Girls.” It is during this time that she had also started exploring the opportunities provided by the internet. She started making comic videos for several online platforms and sites.

A video called, “Blowjob Girl” featuring her as a college girl went viral. It was in 2009 that she auditioned for a role in “The Office,” for which she was selected. Her outstanding performance became one of her best and critically acclaimed works; in fact, it got her to the Forbes’ list of Top 10 Comedians in the World. She also appeared in the original Netflix series “Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt.”

Achievements and awards

Ellie Kemper has been nominated for several categories in the Screen Actors Guild awards. She was also nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a comedy series. Apart from this, she has won several awards, like the Webby award for best actress. She has also won the New York’s Film Critics’ award for best ensemble.
